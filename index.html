Voter Characteristics Visualized

  Who Votes Most Frequently?

In general, it seems like more frequent voters have completed a higher level of education and more income. An interesting thing to note is that more sporadic voters are college graduates than people who always vote. It is important to note that there are more total sporadic voters than any other category. This is why sporadic voters appear to have the largest portion of most categories in the education and income dimensions.

Another piece of information that can be gained from this figure is that people with a higher education level tend to make more money.

Absentee Ballots and Turnout By Region in Virginia (2014-2022)

Note: Some observations in the data move out of the range of the figure due to percent turnout of over 100%.

When viewing the animation through the years, 2020 is the most unique. In 2020, there was a much higher percentage of absentee voters than in other years. This is most likely the result of the COVID-19 pandemic and quarantine guidelines that were implemented during that time.

Another interesting observation is that in 2016 and 2020 there is a noticeable increase in voter turnout. This is likely because certain people prioritize voting in the Presidential elections which occured during those two years.

Which Regions in Virginia Have the Highest Voter Turnout?

Here, it appears voter turnout is highest in the Northeast regions of Virginia and lowest in the Southwest. It almost looks like there is a steady increase in voter turnout along this diaganol moving from Southwest to Northeast.
